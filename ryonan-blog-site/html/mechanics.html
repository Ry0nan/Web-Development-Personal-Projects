Hunting Loop

At the heart of Monster Hunter Rise is the cycle of hunting formidable creatures to gather materials. These materials are then used to craft and upgrade weapons and armor, enabling you to take on even more challenging monsters.

Wirebug Mobility

The Wirebug is a versatile tool that enhances movement and combat. It allows Hunters to perform aerial maneuvers, quickly traverse environments, and execute special attacks unique to each weapon type. This adds a layer of verticality and dynamism to both exploration and battles.

Wyvern Riding

Building upon the Wirebug mechanic, Wyvern Riding lets you temporarily control monsters after weakening them. You can use this to your advantage by directing these creatures to attack other monsters or obstacles, turning the tide of battle in your favor.​

Palamutes and Palicoes

Hunters are accompanied by two types of companions: Palamutes and Palicoes. Palamutes are canine allies that you can ride for faster travel and they assist in combat. Palicoes are feline companions that provide support through healing, setting traps, and other helpful abilities.​

Rampage Mode

Rampage Mode introduces a tower-defense-style gameplay where you defend Kamura Village from waves of attacking monsters. You'll set up fortifications, deploy artillery, and collaborate with other Hunters to protect the village from these onslaughts.​

Switch Skills

Switch Skills allow you to customize your weapon's move set by swapping certain attacks. This feature provides flexibility, enabling you to tailor your combat style to better suit different monsters or personal preferences​

Multiplayer Hunts

Monster Hunter Rise supports cooperative multiplayer for up to four players. Teaming up with others can make hunts more manageable and adds a social element to the game. Communication and coordination are key to taking down the most formidable foes.​
